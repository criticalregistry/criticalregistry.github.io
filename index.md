# The dependency your balance sheet hasn't priced

Critical infrastructure worldwide runs on open source components maintained by individuals with no institutional support. When those maintainers stop, products stop. The Critical Software Risk Register is a public, named, machine-readable map of that risk — built for the organisations who carry it.

## The problem

Every connected product shipped today contains open source components. The supply chain for those components terminates, repeatedly, at individuals. Twenty billion installations of a single networking library. One primary maintainer since 1996. Most of the world's encrypted internet traffic. One full-time developer at the point of its most critical vulnerability. A nation-state attack in 2024 that came within days of shipping a backdoor into SSH on most Linux distributions — executed not through a technical exploit, but through three years of patient social engineering of one exhausted volunteer.

Each incident has had a larger blast radius than the last. The next will be in hardware: automotive firmware, medical devices, industrial control systems where patching is measured in years.
