# About

![Holger Schmidt](/assets/holger-schmidt.jpg){: style="float: right; margin: 0 0 1.5rem 2rem; width: 220px; border-radius: 4px;"}

Safety-critical systems fail in predictable ways, if you know where to look. Two decades in SIL4 rail systems, OT cybersecurity, and autonomous operations across five continents produces that kind of eyesight: the load-bearing assumption nobody is questioning, the dependency with no backup, the structural flaw everyone around the table has agreed not to name.

That is what this registry is. The open source components embedded in critical infrastructure worldwide have a supply chain that terminates, repeatedly, at individuals. The organisations depending on them have not mapped it, have not funded it, and in most cases cannot answer basic questions about it. I built the Critical Software Risk Register because the tools to make that risk visible exist, the regulatory pressure to act on it is arriving, and the entity to assemble them did not.

Nine patents. A Dipl.-Ing. (FH) in electrical engineering and information technology. Dual US-German citizenship. I currently lead business development for Siemens Mobility's digitalisation and cybersecurity portfolio globally. The registry is an independent project.
